# Human Failure Modes in Justice Systems

## Goal
To anticipate and mitigate the ways in which humans interact with the logic of justice, ensuring that the system is perceived as fair even when outcomes are unfavorable.

## 1. The Favorable Outcome Bias
- **Mismatch**: Users often equate "Fairness" with "Winning".
- **Reality**: A fair system must often deliver outcomes that do not favor the user.
- **Mitigation**: Clear expectation setting before any dispute or transaction. Explain the "rules of the game" upfront.

## 2. Evidence Interpretation
- **Mismatch**: Users believe all evidence they provide is equally valid.
- **Reality**: Admissibility and weight of evidence are governed by the Constitution.
- **Mitigation**: Feedback loops that explain *why* evidence was rejected or weighted less.

## 3. Automation Mistrust (or Over-trust)
- **Mismatch**: Viewing AI as either "Perfect" or "Biased".
- **Reality**: AI in Pi Ledger is a "Bailiff" (assistant), not a "Judge".
- **Mitigation**: Visible human checkpoints in the process. Clear labeling of AI-suggested clauses vs. final judgments.

## 4. The Response to Delay
- **Mismatch**: "Justice delayed is justice denied."
- **Reality**: "Justice rushed is justice crushed."
- **Mitigation**: Transparency in the process timeline without promising impossible speeds.
